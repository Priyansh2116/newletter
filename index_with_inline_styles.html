Following our impressive 2nd runner-up finish in IRC 2024, the Team is upgrading the rover to claim the top spot in IRC 2025. We are replacing the Pixhawk and Blackpill controllers with a Nucleo board, centralizing control and improving subsystem integration for a smoother performance.

The new arm design features inverse kinematics, enabling precise maneuvers for tasks like sampling and item retrieval. The biobox has been upgraded for more sophisticated analysis, with increased durability and compactness. Additionally, the team is advancing their navigation system by adopting camera-based autonomous guidance, allowing the rover to interpret its surroundings in real-time and navigate complex obstacles. These enhancements will significantly improve the rover's performance and adaptability, positioning Team's for success in IRC 2025.
With these advancements, Team RUDRA is well-positioned to exceed last year's performance and push the boundaries of rover capabilities in the highly competitive IRC 2025.
